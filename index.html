    // Cabella Data Variable - CABStockD3.js
    var CAB;
    d3.csv("http://www.downeastmarkets.com/data/cabelaHistoric.csv", function(error, data) {
      // assigns Cabella's historical stock info to CAB variable
      CAB = data;
      if (error) {
        console.log(error);
      } else {
        var cabStock = d3.select(".graphCAB").selectAll("div.stockPrice").data(CAB).enter().append("div").attr("class", "stockPrice");
        cabStock.style("height", function(d) {
          return d["Close"] + "px";
        });
      }

Custom WP Theme Development

The above is a homepage mock-up that's part of Fortress Prime's website revamp that we at at Shift Forex are enlisted to build. The development work done will be leveraged to build out a WordPress offering for Shift's "Start-A-Broker" (SAB) business line. The business line currently offers a Drupal web roll-out with its design influenced by a former market leader in the retail FX space (FXCM). I lead, managed and developed the roll-outs for all of Shift's 2015 SAB clients and most (roughtly 80%) of their 2014 clients.

As the lead for Fortress' new site build and the SAB rollouts I've broken down WordPress Theme creation into 3 major steps: Proof of Concept, Convert into WordPress, and Refinement. The following showcases the completion of the first stage. If you compare Proof of Concept with the mock-up you'll find that they're very similar but still needs a little more attention. When I forwarded the dev url to our designer, she mentioned how she'd like to have the background images keep their aspect ratio. To me, that's the last technical hurdle in creating a perfect copy of her design. Any other differences is due to laziness and wanting to move to the second stage. Afterall, time === money;

SquareSpace and Code Injection

Foreign Exchange Industry Conference (FXIC) is another business segment of Shift Forex. The events team occasionally asks me to implement custom features. The most recent was to insert a countdown ticker into the header of their site. The past two events sites have been hosted on SquareSpace. Historically I would have to be develop and maintain a new site for each event. This shift of responsibility has been great for all parties invovled. If you're looking to to create a simple yet elegant site I would look into SquareSpace before hiring a developer. Any developer who's worth their salt has no interest in dealing with the easy stuff anyways.
